# Case Study: Phishing Attack
**Task 5 – Cybercrime Awareness**

## Cybercrime Type: Phishing

---

### What Is Phishing?

Phishing is a form of social engineering where cybercriminals impersonate trusted entities — such as banks, government agencies, or universities — to trick victims into revealing sensitive information like passwords, OTPs, or banking credentials.

---

### Case Scenario: "The Fake HDFC Bank Alert" *(Realistic Fictional Scenario)*

**Riya Sharma**, a 21-year-old college student in Indore, received an SMS appearing to be from HDFC Bank warning that her account would be suspended within 24 hours unless she verified her details immediately. The message included a link that looked nearly identical to the official HDFC website.

---

### How It Happened — Step by Step

1. **Bait:** Riya received an urgent fake SMS with a spoofed sender ID reading "HDFCBANK."
2. **Hook:** The link redirected her to a fraudulent website mimicking HDFC's login page.
3. **Capture:** She entered her Customer ID, password, and the OTP she received on her phone.
4. **Exploit:** Within minutes, attackers used her credentials to transfer ₹47,000 from her savings account.
5. **Disappear:** The fake website went offline; the attackers became untraceable.

---

### Who Is Usually Targeted?

- First-time online banking users and college students
- Senior citizens unfamiliar with digital fraud patterns
- Small business owners managing high-volume UPI transactions
- Anyone who receives communications during stressful moments (exam season, tax deadlines, festive sales)

---

### Consequences

| Dimension | Impact |
|-----------|--------|
| **Financial** | ₹47,000 lost; partial recovery uncertain pending police investigation |
| **Emotional** | Anxiety, shame, and distrust of digital banking platforms |
| **Legal** | FIR filed under Section 66C & 66D of the IT Act, 2000 (India) |
| **Academic** | Riya missed assignment deadlines managing the fallout |

---

### Key Takeaway

> Legitimate banks and institutions **never** ask for your OTP, password, or PIN via SMS, email, or phone call. Always verify URLs manually and report suspicious messages to the **Cyber Crime Portal: cybercrime.gov.in** or call **1930** (India's National Cyber Crime Helpline).
