# 🛡️ Stay Safe Online — Cybersecurity Prevention Checklist
**Task 5 – Cybercrime Awareness | For College Students in India**
*Prepared: March 29, 2026*

---

## General Online Safety

- [ ] **1. Use strong, unique passwords for every account**
  Create passwords with at least 12 characters combining uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols (e.g., `R@iya#2026!secure`). Never reuse the same password across multiple platforms. Use a trusted password manager like Bitwarden or 1Password.

- [ ] **2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) everywhere**
  Turn on 2FA for your email, social media, college portal, and banking apps. Prefer an authenticator app (Google Authenticator, Authy) over SMS-based OTPs wherever possible.

- [ ] **3. Never click links in unsolicited SMS, WhatsApp, or email messages**
  If you receive an urgent message from a "bank," "TRAI," or government body asking you to click a link — stop. Open your browser and navigate to the official website directly. Phishing links often mimic real URLs (e.g., `hdfc-secure-login.com` vs `hdfcbank.com`).

- [ ] **4. Keep your devices and apps updated**
  Enable automatic updates for your phone's OS and all installed apps. Security patches fix vulnerabilities that cybercriminals actively exploit. Avoid using pirated or cracked software — it often contains hidden malware.

- [ ] **5. Use only secured Wi-Fi networks; avoid public hotspots for sensitive tasks**
  Never log into banking apps, college portals, or email on unencrypted public Wi-Fi (cafés, bus stands, railway stations). If you must use public Wi-Fi, use a reputable VPN to encrypt your connection.

- [ ] **6. Review app permissions regularly on your smartphone**
  Check which apps have access to your camera, microphone, contacts, and location. Revoke permissions that are unnecessary. A flashlight app has no reason to access your contacts.

- [ ] **7. Be cautious about what you share on social media**
  Avoid posting your phone number, Aadhaar number, college ID, home address, or daily routine publicly. Cybercriminals use social media profiles to craft targeted scams (spear phishing).

- [ ] **8. Verify before you trust — always check sender identity**
  Before responding to any email or message requesting personal information or action, independently verify the sender through an official phone number or website. No legitimate organisation will ask for your OTP or password.

## 💳 Financial & UPI Safety (Mandatory Tips)

- [ ] **9. Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or bank password with anyone — ever**
  Receiving money via UPI does **not** require entering your PIN. If someone asks you to enter your PIN to "receive" a payment, it is a fraud. Legitimate bank staff, Google Pay, PhonePe, and Paytm representatives will never ask for your PIN or OTP.

- [ ] **10. Set transaction limits and enable SMS alerts on your bank account**
  Log into your bank's app or net banking and set a daily UPI/NEFT transfer limit appropriate to your usage. Enable instant SMS and email alerts for every transaction so you can detect unauthorised activity immediately and act within the golden hour.

- [ ] **11. Use official apps only — download from Google Play Store or Apple App Store**
  Never install banking or UPI apps shared via WhatsApp links or third-party websites. Fake apps mimic real ones and steal your credentials silently in the background.

- [ ] **12. Double-check UPI IDs and QR codes before confirming any payment**
  Before hitting "Pay," verify the recipient's name displayed on the UPI confirmation screen. Fraudsters swap QR codes at shops and payment counters. A misplaced payment is nearly impossible to reverse.

## 🚨 How to Report Cybercrime in India

If you or someone you know has been a victim of any cybercrime — phishing, UPI fraud, identity theft, cyberbullying, or ransomware — report it immediately through official channels:

| Channel | Details |
|---------|---------|
| 🌐 **National Cyber Crime Portal** | [www.cybercrime.gov.in](https://www.cybercrime.gov.in) — File a complaint online 24/7 |
| 📞 **Cyber Crime Helpline** | **1930** — Call to report financial fraud; the sooner you call, the higher the chance of freezing stolen funds |
| 🏛️ **Local Cyber Cell** | Visit your nearest police station and ask for the Cyber Crime Cell |
| 📧 **CERT-In** | [www.cert-in.org.in](https://www.cert-in.org.in) — Report technical incidents and vulnerabilities |

> ⚡ **Time is critical in financial fraud.** Calling **1930** within the first hour of a UPI or banking fraud significantly increases the chances of freezing the transaction before funds are withdrawn.
